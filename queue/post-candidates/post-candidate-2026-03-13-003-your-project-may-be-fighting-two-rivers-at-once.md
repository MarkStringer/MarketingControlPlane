# Your project may be fighting two rivers at once

## Hook

Wild thought: most software projects fail because people think there is one value stream when there are really two.

## Rationale

## Draft

Wild thought:
most software projects are trying to navigate two rivers at once.

One river carries the idea:
attention, sponsorship, politics, enthusiasm, budget.

The other has to carry the product:
users, constraints, discovery, working software, actual value.

These rivers do not naturally meet.

That’s why teams get trapped between flowers and fruit:
what sounded exciting in the room
vs what turns out to be useful in the world.

A lot of delivery pain is just that collision made visible.

This is one of the big ideas behind *Delivering the Impossible*: software gets easier to manage when you can see the difference between backing an idea and building a product.

## Why now

Good awareness post because it introduces a distinctive framework that can make people curious about the book while still offering standalone value.

## Supporting ideas

- Software projects involve two value streams: idea stream and product stream.
- Ideas gain support for emotional or political reasons, not only tested value.
- Delivering real value often means changing the original idea.
- Flowers vs fruit distinguishes attractive ideas from useful products.
