I wrote a book. A chunk of it got written on the Metropolitan line. Every weekday morning for the whole of one May.

Many Mays later, the book came out.

I now have a tiny little bit of experience of how to write a book.

But I realised, I have no idea how to market it.

The last three years, I've done a show at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe. This year, I thought, the show could be "Please Buy My Book!"

Then I thought about it a bit more and realised, wow, that sounds a bit desparate.

So, instead, the show is going to be called *You Can Write a Book!* An 45 minutes about how to write one. Practical. Encouraging. For people who think they've got a book in them.

Here's the thing. If I'm going to tell people they can write a book, I should probably be able to show that I can write one.

So I'm going to write the bare bones of one (again) in May. Starting tomorrow. Every weekday morning. Same method as last time.

And the target is a competition.

The Blue Pencil Agency First Novel Award 2026. Entry closes 31st May. You only need 5,000 words to enter.

I think I can manage that.

The working title is *The Least Interesting Case of Ernesto Klee.*

I'll be posting updates here every day. Like an old-fashioned serial.
