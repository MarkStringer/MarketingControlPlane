# Two streams, one fight

## Hook

A lot of software pain starts with a hidden assumption: once an idea gets funded, the hard thinking is over.

## Rationale

This is a softer promotional candidate that raises awareness of the book by surfacing one of its most distinctive arguments: the tension between the idea value stream and the product value stream. It stays useful rather than salesy.

## Draft

One idea from *Delivering the Impossible* that I keep coming back to is this: software projects usually contain two value streams.

First there is the idea stream:
how something gets attention, sponsorship, budget, and political support.

Then there is the product stream:
how a team discovers what users actually need and turns that into working software.

Those two streams are not the same thing.
In fact, they often fight.

The idea may have been funded because it sounded neat, urgent, strategic, or emotionally compelling.
But the product only becomes valuable when it survives contact with users, constraints, and messy reality.

That is where teams get dragged out of flowers and into fruit.
And usually into the swamp as well.

A lot of project management is really the work of helping people cross that gap without pretending it doesn’t exist.

That gap sits at the heart of the book.

## Why now

Sensible as an awareness-building post because it introduces a core book concept clearly and invites readers into the wider argument without a hard sell.
