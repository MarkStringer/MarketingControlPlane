# Draft: No mirrors, only prisms

---

Jorge Luis Borges said: "Two aesthetics exist: the passive aesthetic of mirrors and the active aesthetic of prisms."

I think he was wrong. I think there are only prisms.

OK, maybe there are fairground mirrors — twisted, manipulated, a distorted vision of what's going on. But it's still you! You can't stop distorting what you see. The moment you decide what to notice, what word to use, what to leave out — you've already changed it. There's no neutral. There's no mirror.

I wrote a book. It's called *Delivering the Impossible* and it's about software project management. One of its central arguments is that point of view is worth 80 IQ points. That seeing a project differently — really differently, not just reframing it — changes what's possible.

Now I realise this is an argument *for* prisms. It took me a whole book to make it.
