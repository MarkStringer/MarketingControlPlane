# Your project has two value streams

## Hook

A lot of software projects struggle for a simple reason: the idea got funded before the product proved it could create value.

## Rationale

This is a strong evergreen post because it uses a distinctive metaphor from the manuscript rather than generic delivery advice. It speaks directly to project and delivery managers and ties to the launch goal of associating the author with memorable software-delivery ideas.

## Draft

One of the most useful ways to see a software project is as two value streams.

First, there’s the idea stream:
how the idea gets attention, support, budget, and momentum.

Then there’s the product stream:
how something actually becomes useful in the hands of users.

Those two streams are not the same.

An idea can move fast because it is exciting, political, well-sponsored, or emotionally persuasive.
A product moves more slowly because reality gets a vote.

Users reveal things.
Constraints appear.
The original concept changes shape.

That’s why projects get tense.
The people who backed the idea often want the product to validate the original story.
But delivery teaches you what the story missed.

A lot of project management is really about handling the gap between those two streams without pretending it isn’t there.

If you manage software delivery, it helps to ask:
- Are we protecting the idea, or improving the product?
- What have users taught us that the original pitch could not?
- Where is the friction between what got funded and what is actually becoming useful?

Deliverability improves when we stop treating the funded idea as the finished truth.

## Why now

Useful at any time because teams constantly confuse approval momentum with real product value. It also introduces a signature book idea in a way that can travel well on LinkedIn.

## Supporting ideas

- Software projects involve two value streams: the idea stream and the product stream.
- The idea value stream is weird and often driven by emotional or political reasons.
- Delivering real value usually means changing the original idea after learning from users.
- Project management means guiding the team through the gap between dream and reality.
