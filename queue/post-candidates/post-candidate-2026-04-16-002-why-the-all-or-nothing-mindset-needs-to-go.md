Many project managers are secretly frustrated. Why? Because they expect a perfect 'biscuit tin and the biscuit' match between plans and reality—a perfect symmetry that rarely exists. It's time to embrace asymmetrical thinking: some features, some time, some knowledge. By accepting partial delivery and actively seeking different viewpoints, we move from projects being endless swamps to flowing value streams.
