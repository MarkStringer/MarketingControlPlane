## Draft

Your stakeholders have been eating the picture on the biscuit tin and wondering why they're still hungry.

The roadmap is not the product.
The spec is not the product.
The diagram, the mockup, the Confluence page, the beautifully formatted Jira epic — none of it is the product.

Only the working software in someone's hands delivers value. Everything else is a description of a biscuit.

The problem is that descriptions are *very convincing*. A good spec feels like a plan. A good plan feels like a thing that's almost done. A thing that's almost done makes the stakeholder feel like it's already in production.

This is why they want everything. Immediately. Exactly as first imagined. And faster than is sensible.

They've already eaten the biscuit in their heads. They just haven't told you.

The only cure is to put working software in front of them — early, incomplete, slightly embarrassing — and make them encounter reality before they've fully committed to the fantasy.

Some value. Possible outcomes. Short steps.

Otherwise you'll spend six months building the tin.
