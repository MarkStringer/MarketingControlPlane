# Draft

David Foster Wallace's biography is called "Every love story is a ghost story."

That made me think: every project is a drama. Not always a love story. But always some kind of story.

They say there are only three reasons people get married: money, love, and revenge. And those are also three very good reasons to start a project.

**Money.** Someone has an idea. They think it'll make money. Classic. Perfectly reasonable. Everyone understands this.

**Love.** Someone has an idea and they just believe in it. Enough other people believe in it too — or at least believe in the person — that it gets funded and started. This is how a lot of good things get built.

**Revenge.** Our competitor is doing this, so we need to do it. Or: our competitor *isn't* doing this — if we can pull it off, we can crush them. Cold, strategic, effective.

Then there are a couple of extras.

**Tragedy.** The last project was a disaster. This one has to succeed. There's a particular energy to these projects. Not always good energy.

**Compliance.** The law says we have to have this software. Not dramatic. Not inspiring. But here we are. And a lot of these projects are what you'd call a "burning platform" — the old system could blow at any minute. The drama is just quieter. More like dread.

Every project has a motive. And the motive shapes everything — the energy, the politics, the way people talk about it in corridors, whether anyone actually cares if it succeeds.

It's worth knowing which story you're in.
