# Draft

I had a friend who wanted to learn to play the guitar.

I told him to get lessons.

He didn't want lessons. Because if he took lessons, he'd have to learn to read music. And Paul McCartney doesn't read music. Paul McCartney was worried that learning to read music would interfere with his creativity.

It felt rude to say it. But I said it anyway.

"You're not Paul McCartney."

Paul McCartney is arguably the most successful composer who has ever lived. His very specific anxieties about his very specific gifts make complete sense — for him.

For everyone else, learning the basics of music — like how to read and write it — is probably fine.

---

I believe everyone is capable of writing a book. That's the name of my Edinburgh Fringe show this August: *You Can Write a Book*.

But "capable" doesn't mean you're going to produce a masterpiece without learning anything. It doesn't mean the basics aren't worth learning. It doesn't mean you're secretly Paul McCartney and the only thing standing between you and genius is the risk of becoming too technically accomplished.

Learn the basics. They won't get in the way of what you want to say. They'll help it survive.
