# Organisations are set up to deal with biscuit tins, not biscuits

As a rule, organisations are set up to deal with descriptions of things. Not the actual things.

## Draft

As a rule, organisations are set up to deal with biscuit tins.

They have processes for making descriptions of things. Governance for approving descriptions. Systems for tracking, updating, and versioning descriptions. Whole departments whose job is to produce and maintain descriptions.

What they are often much worse at is dealing with the actual biscuit. Which has its own rules. Which is initially disappointing. Which isn't yet the thing that was imagined. Which requires attuning rather than approving.

This is one reason software delivery is structurally difficult in large organisations. It isn't just that people confuse the plan with the product — it's that the organisation is genuinely better equipped to manage one than the other.

The description lives in the world that the organisation was built for. The working thing doesn't, yet.
