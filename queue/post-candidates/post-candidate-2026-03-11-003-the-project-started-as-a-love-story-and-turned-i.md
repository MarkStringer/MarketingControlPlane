# The project started as a love story and turned into agriculture

## Hook

Most software projects begin as poetry in the idea stream and end up doing farm work in the product stream.

## Rationale

This is the wilder candidate: more poetic, more metaphor-stacked, and more book-aware, while still grounded in supplied material. It connects two value streams with flowers vs fruit in a vivid way that should stand out on a shorter-form channel.

## Draft

Most software projects have two value streams.

First, the idea stream:
attention, excitement, funding, certainty, politics, hope.

Then the product stream:
users, constraints, trade-offs, learning, working software.

The trouble is that the idea stream can fall in love with flowers.
The product stream still has to grow fruit.

That is where projects become tense.
To build something genuinely useful, the team usually has to change the original idea.
And the people who backed the idea can experience that as betrayal rather than progress.

A lot of delivery pain lives in that gap between the dream that got funded and the thing that will actually help users.

That gap is not failure. It is the work.

That is a big theme in Delivering the Impossible.
