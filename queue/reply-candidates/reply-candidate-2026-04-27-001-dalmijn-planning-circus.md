Criticises the "planning circus" as an illusion of control for managers rather than a realistic strategy for complex work.

The planning illusion isn't just psychological. Once the plan is approved, it becomes a political fact.

Changing it requires admitting that the original estimates were wrong, which is usually true but rarely welcome. Everyone who approved the plan has now committed to a version of the future. Bad news after approval isn't just unwelcome information. It's an accusation.

That's why planning for complex work isn't just inefficient. It's actively harmful. The more uncertain the work, the more damaging it is to pretend certainty at the start, because you've then spent political capital defending a fiction.

The plan doesn't just give managers the illusion of control. It gives everyone involved an obligation they can't easily walk away from.
