# Source summary

A single-line reframe: "Scope doesn't creep. Understanding grows." Neal R. argues that what gets called scope creep is actually the natural expansion of knowledge among stakeholders and team.

# Why reply

The reframe is correct as far as it goes, but it stops one step short of the structural problem. Understanding does grow — and then the commitments made at the start don't grow with it. The team now knows more. The stakeholders are still holding the original ticket. That gap is where the pain comes from, and naming it is more useful than just relabelling the phenomenon.

# Draft reply

Exactly right.

And the next part is the painful one.

When understanding grows, the commitments made at the start don't. The team now knows more about what the project actually needs. The stakeholders are still holding the original ticket.

What gets called scope creep is usually the gap between what was promised when nobody understood it well and what's actually needed now that people do. That gap is real information. The only question is what you do with it.
