'We build the future' is a compelling frame. It is also the one that explains the 70% failure rate mentioned in the same keynote.

The observation that people closest to the problem know more than people closest to the PowerPoint is exactly right. The harder question is whether the people who built the PowerPoint will hear what the people closest to the problem are actually finding out. Often they won't, because it undermines the vision.

The project is a bet. You can build futures, but only if you stay in honest contact with what the project is telling you. That requires treating bad news as data, not as a threat to the plan.
