All of these systemic obstacles have something in common: they were almost certainly visible before the project started, or became visible early on, and nobody stopped to update the commitment.

Tech debt without fix time, competing priorities, cross-team dependencies — these aren't surprises. They're features of the actual environment that the project plan usually ignores. Engineers end up absorbing the gap between what was promised and what the system actually supports.

The question isn't why engineers aren't working harder. It's why the planning process keeps producing commitments that assume a system that doesn't exist.
