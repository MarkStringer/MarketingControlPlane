These three appear on every list of reasons projects fail. They have appeared for decades. That they keep reappearing is the most interesting data point.

Unrealistic planning persists not because project managers lack skill, but because saying "this will take twice as long as you want" is a career risk. Poor communication persists not because people lack tools, but because the news to be communicated is bad and the organisation punishes the messenger. Unclear roles persist because clear accountability on a project that might fail is something nobody volunteers for.

The list identifies symptoms. The cause is the incentive structure. Fix what the organisation actually rewards and the symptoms take care of themselves. Listing the symptoms again, however precisely, does not.
