The "better decisions with less noise" framing puts the emphasis in the wrong place.

Projects don't primarily fail because of poor decisions at the start. They fail because the world changes and the team can't update fast enough — new information arrives, dependencies shift, the original assumptions erode — and the plan stays fixed anyway.

The PM skill that matters isn't cleaner decision-making before the work begins. It's building the habits that let the team adjust as reality diverges from the plan: short cycles, early signals, decisions made on current information rather than last quarter's commitments.

Less noise isn't the goal. Faster learning is.
