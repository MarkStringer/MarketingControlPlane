The gap between how much leaders think they listen and how much they actually do is real. But framing it as a behaviour problem misses the structural layer underneath.

People don't suppress information because they're not brave enough. They do it because they've learned what happens to whoever raises the problem. You get labelled, sidelined, quietly associated with the bad news you delivered. That's not timidity, it's rational.

The fix isn't better conversation techniques. It's changing what happens to bad news when it arrives. Treat it as data and you don't need a culture programme. Make it political and no amount of psychological safety workshops will help.
