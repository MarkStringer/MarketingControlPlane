There's something underneath this that's worth naming.

Certification assumes that project management is reproducible. That there's a proven set of practices you can learn, test, and apply. Which is true, up to a point.

But all projects are swamps. And every swamp is different. The certification maps last year's swamp. It doesn't tell you where the crocodiles are in this one.

The deeper problem isn't that organisations mistake certification for competence. It's that they're looking for a repeatable process for something that's fundamentally unrepeatable. No certified PM will fix that, and neither will the right training, until the organisation understands that delivery requires navigating uncertainty, not eliminating it.

You can't certify your way out of a swamp.
