The disconnect you're describing is real. But I think it's actually two different arguments happening in the same thread.

"Regular folks" often aren't rejecting Scrum. They're rejecting what got implemented under Scrum's name: standups with no slack, retrospectives that changed nothing, sprints that were deadline pressure in a different vocabulary. That's not Scrum. That's waterfall in costume.

Scrum Masters are arguing about how to implement the framework correctly. Practitioners are asking whether any framework can hold up when the problem keeps changing faster than the sprint cycle.

Here's the thing. Both are right, about different things. And neither camp is asking the question that actually matters: what does this particular project need?

The certification well dried up because certifications sold the idea that you could manage the swamp by learning its name. The swamp doesn't care what you call it.
