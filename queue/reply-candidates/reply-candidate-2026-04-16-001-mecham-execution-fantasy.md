# Draft reply

The assumption baked into that statistic is that the plan was sound and execution let it down. But most project plans are made at the point of maximum ignorance — before the team knows enough to know what they don't know.

When the project fails, "poor execution" is a convenient diagnosis because it protects the planning process. Nobody goes back and asks: was this plan ever achievable? Was the scope realistic? Was the deadline invented?

The project is a bet. Every plan is a guess dressed up as a schedule. The ones that succeed aren't the ones where execution matched the plan — they're the ones where the team could navigate when it didn't.
