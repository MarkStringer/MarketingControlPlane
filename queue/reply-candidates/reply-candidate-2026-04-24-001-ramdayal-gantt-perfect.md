"Every project looks perfect in Microsoft Project, until you start."

That is still too generous. The project was wrong before you started — execution just makes it visible.

The Gantt chart is a theory of the future. Most of its inputs are guesses: how long tasks take, what the dependencies actually are, what requirements mean in practice. Those guesses do not become false when work begins. They were false when they were written.

The gap between plan and reality is not a failure of execution. It is what happens when you treat a guess as a fact for long enough that nobody remembers it was a guess. The project is a bet from day one. "Until you start" implies there was a period when the plan was correct. There was not.
