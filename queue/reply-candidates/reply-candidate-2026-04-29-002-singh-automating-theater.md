The question is right. But I'd go one level deeper before answering it.

Before asking whether AI should do a task, ask why the task exists.

A lot of PM admin, the status reports nobody reads, the dashboards permanently stuck on amber, the meeting minutes piling up in a shared drive, exists because the organisation can't face bad news directly. The process is a buffer between what's actually happening and what leadership wants to hear.

Automating that buffer doesn't solve the problem. It makes the theatre run faster.

If half the job is performative, removing the performance doesn't change the underlying dynamic. It removes the friction that occasionally forced someone to name what was actually going on.

Bad news is data. The parts of the PM job worth eliminating aren't the slow ones. They're the ones that exist to manage an organisation's discomfort with uncertainty. Those don't disappear with better tooling.
