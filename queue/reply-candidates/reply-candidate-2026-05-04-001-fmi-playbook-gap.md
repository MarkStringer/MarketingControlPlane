The 24% figure is striking, but I don't think the underlying problem is discipline.

Playbooks are written for the project as planned. Once the project starts diverging from the plan — which it always does — the playbook becomes progressively less useful. It describes a project that no longer exists.

What distinguishes the 2.5% who finish on time and on budget probably isn't stricter adherence to the playbook. It's better habits for navigating the gap between plan and reality: short feedback loops, early surfacing of problems, decisions made on current information rather than the original assumptions.

A playbook can't teach you that. It can only describe what good looked like before things went wrong.
