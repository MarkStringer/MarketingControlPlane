# Source summary

Jordan Cutler argues that estimates routinely get treated as deadlines, creating implicit commitments neither party intended. His prescription: use fuzzier formats (t-shirt sizing, sprint-based estimates) and explicitly flag that a number is a rough guess so it can be referenced later if timelines slip.

# Why reply

The communication fixes are sensible but don't address why the conversion happens. Estimates become deadlines because the estimate was part of the story that justified the project — someone used it to secure sign-off or promise delivery to a customer. Making the number fuzzier doesn't undo that commitment; it delays the collision. The structural issue is that the project is a bet made with whatever was known at the start, and the gap between estimate and actuality is what reality arriving looks like — not a communication failure.
