"Flawed processes also move faster" is the insight this whole post is built on.

It's not specific to AI. Every efficiency tool has this property. Automate a broken feedback loop and you get faster broken feedback. Speed up reporting in a culture that doesn't act on bad news and you get faster ignored reports.

The question "which human decision are we trying to improve?" is a project management question. It's always been a project management question. AI projects fail for the same reasons non-AI projects fail: nobody has agreed what success looks like, or the people who could change course don't want to hear that they need to.

The technology isn't the variable. The clarity is.
