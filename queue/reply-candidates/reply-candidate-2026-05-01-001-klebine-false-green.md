The three tactics work. But they're all still treating this as a reporting problem.

In my experience, the PMs reporting green aren't usually lying to the board. They're lying to themselves. They believe the downstream problems will resolve because they need to believe it. The gap between the plan and reality is uncomfortable, so the plan stays real a little longer.

The discipline isn't better reporting mechanics. It's learning to trust what the project is actually telling you, even when it contradicts the plan you built last month and promised to the sponsor last quarter.

Bad news is data. The sooner it arrives, the cheaper it is.
