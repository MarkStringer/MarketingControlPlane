# Source summary

Issam Farraj advises project managers on handling underestimation mistakes in EPC projects. His six-step framework includes: identify the problem factually, assess contractual position, communicate internally first, develop mitigation scenarios, then — crucially — manage client communication by reframing the problem as "scope clarification or design development" rather than admitting a mistake outright.

# Why reply

The specific advice to reframe underestimation as "scope clarification" is exactly the kind of move that turns a recoverable situation into a trust crisis. Mark's core position — bad news is data, deliver it cleanly — is a direct structural counter to this. The post has real engagement (EPC audience, specific claim) and the disagreement is substantive, not pedantic.

# Draft reply

The reframe-it-as-scope-clarification move is tempting but it almost always backfires. The client eventually finds out what happened, and now they have two pieces of bad news: the original underestimation, and the fact that you dressed it up.

The cleaner path is to lead with facts. Not "we made a mistake" performed as confession, but "here's what we know now that we didn't know then, here's what it means, here's what we're doing about it." Clients absorb bad news far better than most project managers believe. What they can't absorb is discovering the problem was renamed.

Bad news is data. The job is to deliver it clearly, not to find a more palatable label for it.
