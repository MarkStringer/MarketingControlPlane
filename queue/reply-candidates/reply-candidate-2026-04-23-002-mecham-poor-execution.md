"67% of plans fail due to poor execution" — set aside whether that statistic is real. The diagnosis is the issue.

"Poor execution" is what you call it when the outcome does not match the plan. It explains nothing about why. The plan was probably wrong before anyone started executing. The timelines were borrowed from projects that were different. The resources were assumed rather than confirmed. The risks were listed and then filed away.

A plan is a bet. Improving the odds means understanding what you actually know before you commit, not running harder after committing to a bad frame. "Adaptability culture" is the advice you give when it is already too late to change the bet.
