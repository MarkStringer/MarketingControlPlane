The Scrum Master → Agile Project Manager rebrand is interesting, but what changes is the job description, not the underlying challenge.

Someone still has to stand in a room of stakeholders and say "this isn't going to be ready when you think it is" and mean it. Someone still has to translate the difference between what the team thinks they're building and what the client thinks they're getting. Those conversations are hard whether you call the person a Scrum Master, a Project Manager, or an Agile Transformation Lead.

The "embrace AI or fall behind" framing appears reliably every decade, usually attached to whichever technology is current. The teams that actually fall behind aren't the late adopters. They're the ones that use new tools to defer the conversation they were already avoiding.
