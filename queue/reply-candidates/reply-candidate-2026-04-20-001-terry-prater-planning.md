"Proper Prior Planning Prevents Poor Performance" is true as far as it goes, but it doesn't go very far.

Every project is a bet on a map that turns out to be wrong. The plan is a model of the territory, not the territory. The moment execution starts, reality begins handing you corrections — and the quality of those corrections is information you couldn't have had upfront.

The PMs who survive aren't the ones with the most thorough upfront plan. They're the ones who know how to update their model when the corrections come in, and who say clearly what those corrections mean for the schedule, the budget, and the scope.

You can't plan your way out of a swamp. You can only learn to navigate it.
