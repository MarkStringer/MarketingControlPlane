"They're not trying to take advantage. They just don't know where the boundaries are because you never drew them."

On a contained design engagement this is often true. On a complex project it is rarely sufficient.

The problem on most software or transformation projects is not that the boundaries were never communicated. It is that the boundaries were not knowable at the start. The scope is discovered through execution, not defined before it. Requirements turn out to mean something different in practice than they did in the brief. The system the project needs to integrate with is different from what anyone believed. New constraints surface that were invisible at kick-off.

A detailed statement of work is worth having. But on a complex project it is often an agreement about a version of the project that does not yet exist. The scope creep is not a communication failure. It is the project revealing what it actually is.
