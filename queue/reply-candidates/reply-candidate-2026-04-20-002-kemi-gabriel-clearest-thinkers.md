"The clearest thinkers in the room" — yes, but clear thinking includes saying what the data actually shows when the data is bad.

Most projects don't fail because the PM lacked strategic vision. They fail because somewhere in the chain, bad news got softened, deferred, or dressed up as a temporary setback. The PM who navigates ambiguity while managing the narrative upward is not thinking clearly. They're managing impressions.

Bad news is data. The strategic shift that actually matters is toward PMs who treat it that way — who surface the gap between plan and reality at the moment it's cheapest to act on, not after the deadline has passed and the excuses are already prepared.

That's harder than thinking clearly in a room. It requires saying uncomfortable things to people with power over your career.
