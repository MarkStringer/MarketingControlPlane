Monte Carlo does not replace false certainty. It replaces simple false certainty with elaborate false certainty.

The failure on most projects is not that the probability distribution was wrong. It is what happens to the output. "35% chance of hitting the deadline" goes into a status report, reaches the same sponsor who was already ignoring the warning signs, and produces the same decisions.

The model becomes a buffer between what the team actually knows and the conversation that could change something. Better data does not fix that. Someone still has to carry the bad news.
