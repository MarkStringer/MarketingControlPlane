# Source summary

Bonnie Biafore argues that most tools marketed as "project management" software are really just task-management platforms. True project management requires tracking task dependencies and optimising workflows. Without dependency tracking, "the tool isn't doing project management, you're doing project management in your head."

# Why reply

Biafore's observation is correct but the implication — that "doing it in your head" is a problem to be solved — can be pushed further. The mental model isn't a workaround for bad tooling; it is the project. A Gantt chart or dependency tracker is just one frozen projection of that model at a moment in time. The danger isn't PMs who track dependencies mentally. It's PMs and organisations that mistake the chart for the project — and stop updating their mental model because they trust the tool.

# Draft reply

Bonnie — this is right about tools, but I'd push it one step further.

"Doing it in your head" isn't the problem — it's unavoidable. The project only exists as a mental model. The tool is a snapshot of one version of that model at a point in time.

The danger isn't PMs who track dependencies mentally. It's PMs — and organisations — who mistake the chart for the project. A Gantt chart that doesn't change every two weeks isn't managing complexity. It's hiding it.
