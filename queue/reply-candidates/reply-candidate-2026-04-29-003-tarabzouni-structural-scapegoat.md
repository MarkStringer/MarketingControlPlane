Projects are unusually good at manufacturing scapegoats.

Not because leaders are cowards. Because the PM role is built for it: full accountability for outcomes, no authority over the decisions that actually determine those outcomes.

When a project fails, leadership needs a story about why. The PM is structurally ideal. In charge enough to bear responsibility, not in charge of the strategic commitments, the resourcing decisions, or the timelines set before they arrived.

Scapegoating isn't a character failure that good people avoid. It's the predictable end of a structure that separated authority from accountability from the start.

Fix the structure, and you have fewer scapegoats to choose from. Leave it in place, and even the best leaders will reach for one eventually.
