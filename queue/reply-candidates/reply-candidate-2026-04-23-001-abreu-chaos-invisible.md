"If done right, nobody notices" sounds like a compliment. It is a structural trap.

The PM who successfully keeps a project invisible also eliminates their own standing. Nobody invests in preventing a fire they have never seen. Nobody agrees to realistic timelines because they have never watched an unrealistic one collapse — or if they did, they blamed execution rather than the plan that encoded the bad assumptions.

The chaos does not disappear. It accumulates out of sight until the suppression fails. That is the moment everyone notices, but by then the problem is months or years old and the PM is being asked to explain it.
