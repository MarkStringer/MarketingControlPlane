The genuinely hard thing is telling the truth about the plan when the plan was agreed upwards and the sponsor is emotionally invested in it. That requires a specific kind of courage that "thinking like a leader" doesn't quite name. # Draft reply

The "leader not task manager" framing is appealing, and I broadly agree. But it understates where the difficulty actually sits.

It's relatively easy to inspire a team, run a good meeting, and unblock dependencies. The hard part is telling the truth about the plan — especially when the plan was agreed upwards, the sponsor is emotionally invested in it, and the data is starting to suggest it won't work.

That requires something different from leadership in the conventional sense. It requires the PM to be the person who sees what everyone else is avoiding seeing.

Point of view is worth 80 IQ points. The problem is you have to be willing to use it.
