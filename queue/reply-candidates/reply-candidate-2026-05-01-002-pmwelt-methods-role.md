You're right that it isn't the method. But I'm not sure it's the role either.

The deeper problem is that everyone involved — the PM, the sponsor, the client — often misunderstands what a project actually is. A project is a bet. You're committing to a future you can't fully know, with information you don't yet have.

Role clarity doesn't change that. Better methods don't change that. What changes it is building in the habits that let you update the bet as you learn more: short feedback loops, early bad news, decisions made on current reality rather than the original plan.

That's harder to teach than a role description. It requires everyone in the room to accept uncertainty as the starting condition, not as a failure to plan well enough.
