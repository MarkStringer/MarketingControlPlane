## Suggested reading

1. **"Artful Making: What Managers Need to Know About How Artists Work" by Robert Austin and Lee Devin**
   This book explores how the principles of theatrical production can be applied to business and project management, making it relevant given the emphasis on themes like live performance, creative process, and the intersection of art and market.

2. **"The Gift: Creativity and the Artist in the Modern World" by Lewis Hyde**
   Hyde's exploration of gift economies and the nature of creative work directly connects to new themes like gift economy, creative economy, and craft. It offers a profound reflection on how creative works are shared and valued differently from commercial objects.

3. **"The War of Art: Break Through the Blocks and Win Your Inner Creative Battles" by Steven Pressfield**
   This book focuses on overcoming creative obstacles and fear, providing insight into tackling themes such as creative permission, shitty first drafts, and the balance between rules and conventions in creative practice.

4. **"Understanding Comics: The Invisible Art" by Scott McCloud**
   McCloud's analysis of comics as a storytelling medium relates to the themes of language and power, and participation. It also introduces concepts of grammar and context-dependent processes in a visual format, which can inspire new insights into communication and storytelling methodologies.

5. **"Impro: Improvisation and the Theatre" by Keith Johnstone**
   This work delves into the world of improvisation, examining themes like participation, facilitation, and status, offering a fresh perspective on engaging with creative processes and overcoming the fear associated with live performance and creative participation.

6. **Wildcard: "Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman**
   Kahneman's exploration of the dual systems of thought provides surprising insights into themes like self-deception, suggestion, and arguing-as-alignment, illustrating how cognitive biases and heuristics can shape team communication and creative decision-making, echoing collective-hypnosis metaphors.
