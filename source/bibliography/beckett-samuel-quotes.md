# Samuel Beckett — Quotes

## Quote 1

> "The expression that there is nothing to express, nothing with which to express, nothing from which to express, no power to express, no desire to express, together with the obligation to express."

**Source:** "Three Dialogues with Georges Duthuit," *Transition*, 1949.

**Note:** On compulsion — the obligation to express exists independent of ability, material, or desire. You don't choose to write; you find yourself writing despite having no good reason to.

---

## Quote 2

> "Ever tried. Ever failed. No matter. Try again. Fail again. Fail better."

**Source:** *Worstward Ho*, 1983.

**Note:** On iteration — the goal is not eventual success but a better quality of failure. Honest in a way most writing advice isn't.

---

## The two quotes together

The first explains why you're doing it at all. The second explains what you do after it goes badly. Together: you express because you must, and when it fails you fail better next time. Neither quote promises a good outcome. Both make the activity worthwhile anyway.
