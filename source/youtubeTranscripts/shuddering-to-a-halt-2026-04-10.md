# Shuddering to a Halt — should you be doing this? (2026-04-10)

## Summary

Mark reflects on self-doubt as a structural phenomenon — "shuddering to a halt" — triggered by the word "should". Using his own show as the example (is he qualified to tell others they can write books, having only written one?), he identifies "shoulds" as a form of self-competition that gets in the way of doing anything. Getting things done often requires ignoring these shoulds.

## Key ideas

- "Shuddering to a halt" — the sudden freeze of self-doubt when doing something new.
- The word "should" as the signal: *should* I be doing this? *Should* I be allowed?
- We often end up competing with ourselves rather than cooperating.
- Getting anything done quite a lot of the time involves ignoring these shoulds.
- Cooperation and competition are often closer together than we think — applies to our relationship with ourselves too.
