# Finding a Sustainable Pace

## Summary
This short video reflects on the tension between two inner voices during book revision: one urging steady, sustainable progress, and the other demanding speed, intensity, and constant effort. It argues that creative work only really advances when it is done at a pace that can be maintained, and links that idea directly to the show *The Biscuit Tin and the Biscuit* and to project-management thinking about sustainable pace.

## Key ideas
- Creative work often produces an internal conflict between patience and self-criticism.
- The urge to push much harder can feel virtuous, but may actually lead to burnout or avoidance.
- Sustainable pace is not laziness; it is the condition that makes long creative projects possible.
- Revision, book marketing, and show development all depend on finding a rhythm that can be maintained over time.
- Ideas borrowed from project management can be applied directly to creative work.

## Notable phrases
- the angel on one shoulder and the devil on the other
- just carry on at the pace you're carrying on at
- why can't you rip through this much faster than this?
- finding a sustainable pace
- a way of doing this that you can carry on doing for a long time
