# We Gotta Get Outta Here

## Summary
This transcript develops the idea of **escape**: to discover the value of software, it has to get out of the development team, out of the organization, and into the hands of the people who will actually use it. It also argues that even failed or partial attempts at release are still valuable, because they reveal the organizational barriers, constraints, and surrounding "swamp" that are preventing delivery.

## Key ideas
- You only really learn the value of software when it reaches actual users.
- Software must escape not just the team, but ideally the whole organization.
- Early attempts at release may stall before reaching users.
- Even unsuccessful release attempts are informative because they show what is blocking delivery.
- Trying to release software teaches you about the organizational swamp around the work.

## Notable phrases
- the software needs to escape the organization
- get it into the hands of the people who are going to finally use it
- trying to get this software to escape will teach you lots and lots about the organization
- it'll teach you what is surrounding you that's stopping you getting your software out into the world

## Possible marketing uses
- Post about why release attempts are valuable even when they do not fully succeed.
- Post about the difference between completed development and delivered value.
- Reply to discussions about MVPs, phased rollout, or internal resistance to release.
- Connect the idea of escape to the book's wider metaphor of the swamp.
- Use as a hook for posts about learning from blocked delivery rather than hiding it.
