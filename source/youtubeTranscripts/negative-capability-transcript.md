# Negative Capability

## Summary

Prompted by a LinkedIn comment about what's involved in being a project manager, Mark recalls the concept of negative capability from the conclusion of Delivering the Impossible. He reads the Keats quote directly, then connects the idea to both project management (sometimes you just have to be comfortable not knowing) and to his Edinburgh Fringe show You Can Write a Book (being comfortable with uncertainty is also essential for writers). He reflects that our education system — and wider culture — doesn't give us a safe space to practise negative capability, so people who get good at it have usually had to find their own way there.

## Key ideas

- Negative capability: "being capable of being in uncertainties, mysteries, doubts without any irritable reaching after fact and reason" (Keats)
- Project managers sometimes have to simply be comfortable with not knowing a lot of things
- Writers face the same challenge — including not knowing when your book will be published
- There's no obvious space in education or culture where we learn to practise negative capability
- Getting comfortable with it takes time; most people who are good at it have had to figure it out themselves

## Notable phrases

- "capable of being in uncertainties, mysteries, doubts without any irritable reaching after fact and reason"
- "not get irritable about it, not reach after fact and reason"
- "a safe space to experiment with negative capability"
