So, the idea for my book, Delivering the Impossible, which it looks like I'm going to have actual physical copies of very soon, um, is a whole bunch of different points of view, it's different ways of seeing project management. And the idea is rather than kind of giving you a prescriptive um way of dealing with projects, lots of books do that say like you know this is the one way if you do this way everything will be fine. My experience is that that's not true that kind of there are so many different factors involved you can't really try and follow one kind of particular set of methods. And so what I tried to do is to just show a whole bunch of um ways of seeing projects that are helpful and then actually show a bunch of ways of seeing projects that aren't helpful but are actually very common like um delivering on time and to budget and delivering what you initially said you were going to deliver. Those sound like compelling things, don't they? Um and one of the things I explain in the book is is why they're not.
