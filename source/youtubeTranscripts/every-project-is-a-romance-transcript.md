# Every Project is a Romance

## Summary

This video opens with a David Foster Wallace quote — that every love story is a ghost story — and uses it as a jumping-off point to argue that every project is also some kind of romance. Just as relationships carry the ghosts of past relationships, projects carry the weight of what came before.

The video identifies the classic motivations for a relationship (money, love, revenge) as equally valid motivations for a project. It also draws on the language of disaster movies — burning platforms, sinking ships — as another category of project motivation: not desire, but fear that something bad will happen if the project doesn't go ahead.

The central argument is that there is always some kind of drama behind why a project exists, whether it is passionate desire, fear, or jealousy (seeing that someone else has something like it). Understanding that drama is essential: without it, the project won't make sense.

## Key ideas

- Every love story is a ghost story (attributed to David Foster Wallace): relationships carry the ghosts of past relationships.
- Every project is some kind of romance — a story with emotional stakes.
- The classic reasons for marriage (money, love, revenge) map well onto why projects happen.
- Disaster movie language (burning platforms, sinking ships) represents the fear-based motivation: something bad will happen if we don't do this.
- There is always a drama in the background that caused the project to happen.
- That drama may be positive (passionate desire) or negative (fear, jealousy).
- If you don't find out what the drama is, the project won't make much sense.
