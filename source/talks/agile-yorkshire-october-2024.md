# Delivering the Impossible — Agile Yorkshire

## Summary

A talk covering the major frameworks from *Delivering the Impossible*, structured around audience-picked topics (written on cards) interspersed with jokes on separate cards. Topics covered: SHIP (agreed activity / pirate ship), BET (all projects are bets, hiding hand, nonsense accumulators), ESCAPE (getting software to users changes the project dynamic), STREAM (value streams in software vs manufacturing), SOME/ALL (symmetrical vs asymmetrical thinking, Ignacio Matte Blanco), COMMIT (commitment and consistency, foolish commitments, the robot dog).

## Structural notes

- Uses the **four-question intro**: Why me? Why you? Why this? Why now?
- Jokes are kept deliberately separate from content — written on cards, picked by audience members at random. Non-sequitur, pun-based.
- Topics also picked by audience from cards — creates the impression of looseness while covering prepared material.
- Ends each section by inviting the audience to pick the next joke or topic.

## Key ideas

- Changing your point of view can make you smarter (Alan Kay: "point of view is worth 80 IQ points") — or dumber if you change it the wrong way or refuse to change.
- All projects are bets. Much of project management is pretending they aren't.
- The "hiding hand": everyone involved in a project suppresses its problems because they want the project to happen.
- Project management theater: doing all the PM stuff as if it guarantees success, when it doesn't.
- A nonsense accumulator: a project that has no chance of success because too many of the required things will never happen.
- The Wi-Fi wall of shame: make a problem visible and it gets fixed.
- Agreed activity / scrubbing the decks: doing things that look like work but don't move the project forward. Avoiding the pirate ship.
- Escape: getting software into users' hands changes the power dynamic completely. The people berating you for being late stop being your bosses; the users who want it become your bosses.
- The robot dog (Cialdini): commitment and consistency is used to manipulate people. But: "a foolish commitment is the hobgoblin of little minds."
- Symmetrical thinking (same, all, forever, exact match) vs asymmetrical thinking (some, different, for a while, inexact match). Agile as injecting asymmetry.
