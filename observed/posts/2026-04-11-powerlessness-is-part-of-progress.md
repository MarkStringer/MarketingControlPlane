Patrick Stewart once said the real job of an actor is getting humiliated at auditions. Not performing. Getting rejected, over and over, for reasons that have nothing to do with your ability. I think the same is true of writing. Once you get some movement, you hit a wall of things you can't control. The cover isn't what you wanted. The price isn't what you wanted. The books are in a warehouse somewhere and you don't know if they'll arrive. You want to look like you know what you're doing. You don't. That feeling of powerlessness isn't a sign that something has gone wrong. It's part of the business of progress. In writing, in project management, in any endeavour worth taking seriously. Not many people are going to tell you that.
