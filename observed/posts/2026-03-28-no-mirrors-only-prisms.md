# No Mirrors, Only Prisms

My new show — *You Can Write a Book* — is about writing a book. You writing a book. And part of what I'm doing to prepare for it is reading books by other people on how to write books.

So I've been reading Jorge Luis Borges. Apologies for the appalling pronunciation. But the first line I wrote down from that book is this:

*"Two aesthetics exist: the passive aesthetic of mirrors and the active aesthetic of prisms."*

I hate to disagree with you so early on in the book, Mr Borges. But I'm not sure that first one really does exist.

I don't think you can passively mirror the world. I think anybody who writes about the world puts it through a prism. And if you're trying to say "oh yeah, I'm just mirroring the world" — you're either fooling yourself or you're trying to fool other people.

Maybe a fairground mirror. You can hold up a fairground mirror to the world: a twisted, manipulated vision of what's going on. But I think it's unavoidable. When you try and write about the world, you end up writing about yourself.

There are no mirrors. Only prisms.
