title: Every book on project management is a lie
---

Every book on project management is a lie the author knows they're telling. Not maliciously. Structurally.

People seek project management guidance during crises, not during smooth operations. Authors promise certainty through titles like "The Complete Guide," knowing full well that real-world variables — stakeholders, timelines, organisational culture — remain unpredictable. Both readers and authors participate in this mutual understanding.

Extended to hiring: clients need reassurance, PMs must project confidence, yet both understand that the plan won't survive contact with reality.

Alternative offer: transparency, focus, and some new ideas that come out of a lot of experience. Honest acknowledgement of uncertainty and risk. Delivering the Impossible deliberately highlights this contradiction rather than concealing it.
