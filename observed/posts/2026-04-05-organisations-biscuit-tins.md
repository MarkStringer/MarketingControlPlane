# Organisations and biscuit tins

A key idea in my book. As a rule, organisations are set up to deal with biscuit tins. They have processes for making descriptions of things. Governance for approving descriptions. Systems for tracking, updating, and versioning descriptions. Whole departments whose job is to produce and maintain descriptions. What they are often much worse at is dealing with biscuits. Biscuits have their own rules. Their own needs. Early versions of a prototype biscuit are going to be disappointing. They aren't yet anything like the thing that was imagined. Real, new things require improving many times before they get to approving. This is one reason software delivery is difficult in large organisations. It isn't just that people confuse the plan with the product — it's that the organisation is genuinely better equipped to deal with biscuit tins, rather than biscuits. The description lives in the world that the organisation was built for. The working thing doesn't, at least not yet. Delivering the Impossible: Successful Ways of Seeing Software Project Management by Mark Stringer - Apress
