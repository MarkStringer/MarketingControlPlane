This is right, and it's a very hard sell to anyone who isn't already a practicing project manager. Most sponsors "know" what project management is: on time, to budget. You're not going to change that belief by arguing about metrics.

The only thing that changes it is being hypnotised by working software that's actually valuable — to their customers, to their organisation — arriving regularly and visibly. The practical answer isn't to replace the red things with the green things.

It's to deliver the green things while still nodding at the red things. Keep putting working software in front of users. Make sure the people who matter can see what it's doing for them.

The Agile Manifesto almost says this: we acknowledge the value on the right (red), we just value the left more (green). We're not abolishing on time and to budget. Just not letting it be the only thing. The belief shifts when the experience shifts. Not before.

I go into this in detail in Chapter COMMIT of my book "Delivering the Impossible."
