It's a good point, well made.

I think for me, the challenge isn't saying "No" - but not saying "No way! Are out of your mind?"

A little bit more seriously. I saw someone recently saying that if there are a bunch of things that are ALL priorities, then there are ALL in tension.

Rather than saying "NO" - or "Not if we had until the heat death of the universe" you can say "Well, if delivering this is a priority - and delivering that is also a priority - they actually are in tension. If we start to do one, that will affect our ability to deliver the other."

This is like screaming "NO" but at a frequency inaudible to clients.
