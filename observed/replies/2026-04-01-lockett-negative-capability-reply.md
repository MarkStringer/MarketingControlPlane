# Reply text

John Keats called it "negative capability."

"Being in uncertainties, mysteries, doubts, without any irritable reaching after fact and reason."

He was talking about a creative response. Which we tend to imagine as something detailed and exact — the finished poem, the precise plan, the date pulled from somewhere solid.

But Keats is describing something different. A creative response that is: yes, we don't know yet. And that's OK. And we keep going.

I think we all know about "the irritable reaching."

The stakeholder who needs a date before there's a date to give.

The plan produced not because anyone knows what the plan is, but because the uncertainty is making people twitchy.

Every one of the bullet points you mention requires the same thing.

Staying comfortable with the tension of uncertainty.
Not flinching.
Not producing false certainty to make the discomfort go away.

The organisations that perform at a high level aren't just the ones with execution leaders.

They're the ones where someone is allowed to say "we don't know yet" — and that's treated as information, not failure.
