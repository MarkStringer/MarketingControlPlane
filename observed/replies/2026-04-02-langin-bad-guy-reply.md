# Reply text

I was at big-room planning. I'd been hearing all day that the "interim solution" was dead — user research had shown it wouldn't work for a substantial proportion of users.

At the wrap-up, the product owner said - why don't you do the summary Mark? Oh yeah! Sure! Why not?

I said: "The main thing that's come out of today is that the interim solution is dead."

The people who'd been telling me all day it was dead reacted as if I'd just said black was white, down was up and from now on we should walk everywhere on our hands.

"We can't say that yet."

"We need more data."

I felt ever so slightly exposed.
