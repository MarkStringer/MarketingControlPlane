# Source summary

Samuel Boateng Osei: one of the biggest myths in project management is that more meetings improve communication. They don't — they create the illusion of alignment.

# Reply posted

Thank you for this Samuel. I think you're obviously right - more meetings doesn't automatically equal better communication.

There are some things that I try to do to make my meetings more effective, so that they do work for communication. But they also give people the time and energy to get things done outside of meetings.

1. They start and end on time - people will come to your meetings if they know they start and end on time.

2. They do one single thing. This gives us a chance in that meeting to do that thing well. In planning we plan - we don't do analysis or prioritisation. In retro we talk about how the sprint went.

3. Some meetings, we make them "law of two feet" (an open space idea) - if you're not contributing, and you're not getting anything out of the meeting, then it's OK to leave.
