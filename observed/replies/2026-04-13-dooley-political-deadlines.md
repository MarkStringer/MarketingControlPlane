I had a very similar conversation on a commercial project.

Boss: I need you to tell me the project will be finished by the New Year.

Me: I can't promise that because legal hold everything up by a month.

Boss:

Me:

Boss: What I've decided to hear is that the project will be finished by the New Year.

Those dates don't mean what you think they mean. They're magical totems.

So it was declared "finished" when it was 25% done? So what? Well, yes there have been some minor teething troubles... And of course, there are continuing improvements.

A substantial proportion of people, many who should know better fall for this atavistic, deadline dance every time.

All you need to do is to make sure you're not the one in the wicker man when they burn it.
