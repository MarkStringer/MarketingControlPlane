# Reply text

I had an idea that didn't make it into my first book "Fried Egg Agile."

This is based on a thing I saw on the telly. When Rick Stein interviews chef's, he says "fry me an egg."

He says the duff chefs panic. Because they're worried about how Stein fries an egg, or the "correct" way to do it.

The good guys (and gals) shrug and say "I don't know how you do it, this is how I do it."

# Notes

The "fry me an egg" idea — about confident authenticity vs. anxious mimicry of the "correct" way — is a strong standalone concept. Connects to the new book's argument obliquely: bad project management is often mimicry of a methodology rather than genuine responsiveness to the situation.
