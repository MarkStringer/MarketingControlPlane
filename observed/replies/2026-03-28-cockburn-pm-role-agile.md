Interesting to see that visibility sits some way away from the project manager in this model — or have I misread it?

For me, visibility is close to the heart of what a PM does. Not just communication — reflection too. And goals, because what's actually achievable keeps changing throughout a project. Part of the job is noticing that, and making sure it gets communicated — upwards, downwards, all the way through.

For me, the central paradox is that the data most useful to a project is usually the data most threatening to the people funding it. A lot of what project managers actually do is helping sponsors treat that signal as something to act on, rather than something to manage, minimise or explain away.

This is about to get harder. AI doesn't perform evenly. It will be transformative in some areas. It will fail in others. In some areas, it will fail dangerously. Sponsors have already bet heavily. Some of those bets are going to turn out to be wrong — and they're going to be sitting on evidence of that. The PM who can help them face it, and steer toward what's actually working, is doing something that no tool replaces.
