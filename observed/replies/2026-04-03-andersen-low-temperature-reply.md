# Reply text

Thanks Randi.

This really resonates with my experiences - we don't have to like each other.

We do have to respect each other and show it.

And if there are genuine conflicts we need to surface them.

We don't all have to agree - but we have to agree that we're not agreeing when we're not agreeing.

There's this kind of non-chest-beating, relaxed kind of discussion that I want in planning meetings, and then it leaks into other meetings, which only comes after the kind of measures that you describe.

It's low temperature.

"That thing's too big, we need to split it up."
"Those things are small, we can do three of them together."
"OK, but how are we going to test this?"
"Here's what we did this week."
