# Message house

## Delivering the Impossible (book)

### Core message
Software projects become more deliverable when we learn to see them in smarter ways.

### Pillars
1. Point of view matters.
2. Ideas and products fight.
3. User research reveals the swamp.
4. Projects are bets, not certainties.
5. “All / same / forever” thinking is dangerous.
6. Bad news should be turned into action.

### Signature phrases
- point of view is worth 80 IQ points
- deliver the possible, not the fantasy
- stop smelling the flowers and grow the fruit
- all projects are swamps
- bad news is data
- the project is a bet

## You Can Write a Book (show)

### Core message
There are no mirrors, only prisms. Your distortions are different from anybody else's — that's not a flaw, it's the reason to write.

### Pillars
1. There is no passive aesthetic. You can't record without transforming.
2. The first draft is a tenth of an arse — and that's fine, it's a start.
3. Writing is hypnotising yourself into believing it's worth doing.
4. You need another reader to see the gap between what you wrote and what you meant.
5. You are already a writer. You just proved it.

### Signature phrases
- there are no mirrors, only prisms
- your distortions are different from anybody else's
- the first draft is a tenth of an arse
- writing is hypnotising yourself into believing it's worth doing
- you have a prism, therefore you are a writer

### Bridge to the book
Both products share the same underlying idea from different angles: point of view is the only thing you have, and learning to use it deliberately is the whole skill — whether you're managing a software project or writing a book.
