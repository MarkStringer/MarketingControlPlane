# Launch goals

## Product 1: Delivering the Impossible (book)

### Goal 1
Increase awareness of *Delivering the Impossible* on social media.

### Goal 2
Discuss the issues that arise in the book in relation to:
- project management
- seeing things in different ways
- delivering software

### Desired outcomes
- Establish clear association between Mark Stringer and distinctive software-delivery ideas.
- Use memorable metaphors from the book.
- Join current conversations without becoming generic “hot take” content.
- Keep posts grounded in the manuscript.

### Constraints
- Avoid bland methodology advocacy.
- Avoid overclaiming certainty.
- Tie posts back to the manuscript wherever possible.

## Product 2: You Can Write a Book (Edinburgh Fringe show)

### Goal 1
Build awareness of the show before Edinburgh Fringe (August).

### Goal 2
Use the show as an indirect marketing vehicle for *Delivering the Impossible* — the audience experience and branded notebook/pen do this work, not direct promotion.

### Goal 3
Generate content from the show itself: behind-the-scenes, development process, ideas from the script, audience reactions.

### Desired outcomes
- Establish Mark Stringer as someone with interesting things to say about writing, creativity, and starting things — not just project management.
- Create a pre-Fringe content arc that builds curiosity about the show.
- Leave audience members as advocates: they experienced something, they have a branded notebook.

### Content phases
- **Pre-Fringe (now–July)**: Show development posts, ideas from the script (prisms/mirrors, tenth of an arse, writing as hypnosis), behind-the-scenes, “why I'm doing this”
- **During Fringe (August)**: Live content, audience reactions, short-form video if possible
- **Post-Fringe**: Reflection, what worked, what the audience wrote

### Constraints
- Don't make show content feel like book promotion — the indirect approach is the point.
- Don't overclaim the show's reach before it's happened.
- Cross-promotion should feel natural: shared themes, not shared plugs.
